About NorWave

Welcome to NorWave, your gateway to Norwegian cinema. Our mission is to promote Norwegian films internationally, introducing audiences around the world to the unique storytelling, landscapes, and perspectives that Norwegian cinema has to offer. With a rich history of artistic innovation, Norway’s film industry has captivated global audiences with thought-provoking narratives and visually stunning productions. NorWave celebrates this heritage by spotlighting both emerging talents and established filmmakers who continue to shape Norway’s cinematic voice.

The Story Behind "NorWave"

The term NorWave was first coined in 1997 during the prestigious Cannes Film Festival, where Norway made a remarkable re-entry into international cinema with two groundbreaking films: Budbringeren by Pål Sletaune and Insomnia by Erik Skjoldbjærg. Both films marked the debut of their directors, and Budbringeren went on to win the Mercedes-Benz Award, signaling the start of a new chapter in Norwegian film.

NorWave is a blend of “Norway” and “Wave,” referencing the waves in cinema history that represent the rise of a new generation of filmmakers bringing fresh perspectives and innovative storytelling to the screen. Much like the French New Wave in the 1960s, the NorWave symbolized a creative renaissance in Norwegian film, inspiring a renewed interest both domestically and internationally. Originally used by the Norwegian Film Institute to promote Norwegian cinema, the term gained traction among international film critics and audiences alike.

Though the NorWave movement waned for several years, it resurfaced in 2006, a standout year for Norwegian film. Three Norwegian films, Uro by Mathias Faldbakken, Den brysomme mannen by Jens Lien, and Slipp Jimmy fri by Christopher Nielsen, were featured in Cannes. Additionally, Bobbie Peers won the Short Film Palme d'Or for his film Sniffer, reaffirming Norway’s creative presence on the world stage.

Our Mission

At NorWave, we honor this legacy by fostering a global appreciation for Norwegian films, from thrillers and dramas to documentaries and animated works. We believe that Norwegian cinema has a unique voice in world cinema—one that speaks to universal themes through the lens of a distinctive culture. NorWave is committed to connecting audiences with the vibrant world of Norwegian film, celebrating the achievements of its filmmakers, and paving the way for future generations to share their stories on an international stage.

Thank you for being part of NorWave. We invite you to explore, watch, and become inspired by the creativity and diversity of Norwegian cinema.
